Architecture Layers Explained

This architecture is a high-level, logical representation of how UK government digital services interact. It is organised into layers, each showcasing a specific role in delivering citizen-facing services:

Digital Channels

These are the primary touchpoints for citizens, including GOV.UK websites, mobile apps, chat services, and trusted third-party solutions. They provide the front-end experience where users access information and complete tasks.

Interaction Layer

This layer acts as the bridge between citizen-facing channels and core systems. It includes services such as GOV.UK OneLogin for secure authentication, GOV.UK Pay for payments, GOV.UK Notify for communications, and APIs that enable integration across public and private sectors. Planned components like GOV.UK Digital Backbone will simplify interaction between government organisations.

Business Logic

This layer contains the operational systems that deliver essential government services, such as HMRC for tax, DVLA for driver records, DWP for Universal Credit, and HMPO for passports. Each government organisation owns and runs its business logic and processes, optimising them based on user needs and policy requirements. These systems apply the rules and workflows that underpin public services, ensuring they remain compliant, efficient, and citizen focused.

Data Layer

This layer ensures secure and efficient data exchange. It includes operational platforms like Whitehall CMS, the powerhouse behind GOV.UK content, and the Data Marketplace, which will enable better data sharing across departments and sectors.

Infrastructure

At the foundation are the National Digital Exchange and cloud services, providing scalability, resilience, and interoperability. This infrastructure supports the entire architecture, ensuring services remain reliable and future ready.
